Josef Müller-Brockmann (May 9, 1914 – August 30, 1996) was a Swiss graphic designer and educator.

He is considered to be a key player in the Swiss School of international Style. He was influenced by the ideas of several different design and art movements including Constructivism, De Stijl, Suprematism, and the Bauhaus.

Perhaps his most recognized work was done for the Zurich Town Hall. The work is graphic, rather than illustrative. Some critics say these posters created a mathematical harmony, which reflected the harmony of music.
